Project Overview


2020 - the beginning of the Pandemic. Unprecedented restrictions of movements and lockdowns. What do most people do when they are stuck at home?

Watch "The Office".

According to Nielsen, "The Office" had more than 57 billion minutes streamed in 2020. Why is the show so popular even after nearly a decade from its original airing? Can data science unravel the mystery?

Analytics

We scraped the website https://transcripts.foreverdreaming.org for scripts of the entire show and analysed them by:
  • number of lines each character from the show spoke;
  • the sentiment of the lines spoken; and
  • ranked each episode by their rating.


If you, like us, would like to watch more of The Office, we trained the RNN Text Generation model on four more characters Andy, Dwight, Jim, and Pam and generated a screenplay amongst them at the end. We hope you enjoy the machine generated version of The Office!

Scripted Line Count: Bar Chart Race





In this animated bar chart, we see how each character’s cumulative lines progress across each season and episode.

Note that Michael consistently holds the lead, with more lines than any other character, even with leaving at the end of season 7, and coming back for just 2 lines in the finale. Additionally, Andy’s cumulative line count has him climbing the chart. And clearly Dwight, Jim and Pam are the leads of the show outside of Michael, of course.

Main Character Sentiment By Season





“...what makes The Office so great is this tone of naturalism, realism, groundedness…”
-Brent Forrester, guest. “Mafia.” Office Ladies

Using VADER Sentiment Analysis, a score between -1 and 1 was generated for each line of script. Sentiment analysis scores text based on polarities i.e. positive (1) and negative (1). Of the four central characters (Michael, Dwight, Pam, and Jim) we can see that season over season, their sentiment stays just above zero; meaning the average score of their lines during the run of the show are on the positive side of natural. This aligns with The Office writer, Forrester in his assessment of what makes the show so great

Top Writers of The Office





Individual Writers of each episode have a powerful impact on the tone of the show and how the episode is received.

Filtering the writers to the top contributors, five or more episodes, we can review the average rating and sentiment score of their work. Greg Daniels, the original executive producer and show runner, has the highest average rating at 8.59 out of 10. Michael Schur has the highest sentiment rating at .1642. M. Schur goes on to create shows like Parks and Rec, Brooklyn 99, and The Good Place which are all much more upbeat than The Office. The Office launched the careers of many writers who go on to influence the landscape of 21st Century television
